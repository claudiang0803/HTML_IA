What is breast cancer?

Breast cancer is a disease in which malignant cells form in the tissues of the breast

Signs of breast cancer may include: 

  • Lump in the breast or underarm
    • 

  • Swelling or thickening of all or part of the breast
    • 

  • Dimpling or skin irritation of breast skin
    • 

  • Localized, persistent breast pain
    • 

  • Redness, scaliness, or thickening of the nipple

    • Why should you care about breast cancer?

  • Approximately 1 in 8 women will develop breast cancer in their lifetime.
    • 

  • 85% of the breast cancer diagnoses occur in women with no prior family history.
    • 

  • It is estimated that 2,650 men will be diagnosed with breast cancer in the United States this year.
    • 

  • 93% of women with early breast cancer diagnosis survive beyond 5 years.
    • 

  • There are over 3.8 million breast cancer survivors in the United States.
